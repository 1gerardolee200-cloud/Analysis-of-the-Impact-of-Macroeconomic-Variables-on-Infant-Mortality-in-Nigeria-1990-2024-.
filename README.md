# Analysis of the Impact of Selected Macroeconomic Variables of Infant Mortality in Nigeria (1990–2024).


## Research Objectives

1. Examine the impact of income per head (GDP per capita) on infant mortality in Nigeria.

2. Assess the impact of capital government health expenditure on infant mortality.

3. Determine the impact of access to electricity on infant mortality.

4. Evaluate the contribution of health-specific aid to reducing infant mortality.


## Methodology

- Research Design: Ex-post facto research design

- Data Type: Annual time-series data

- Period Covered: 1990–2024

- Estimation Technique: ARDL and ECM

- Software:

  - EViews

  - Microsoft Excel


## Variables

### Dependent Variable

- Infant Mortality Rate (IMR)


### Independent Variables

- GDP per Capita (GDPPC)

- Capital Health Expenditure (CHE)

- Access to Electricity (ACE)

- Health-Specific Aid (AID)

## Key Findings

- Access to electricity significantly reduced infant mortality.

- Health-specific aid contributed positively to infant survival.

- GDP per capita showed a weak but beneficial effect.

- Capital health expenditure was statistically insignificant, suggesting spending inefficiencies.

- The ECM confirmed a long-run relationship among the variables.
